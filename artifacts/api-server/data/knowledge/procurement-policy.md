# Procurement Policy

## Overview
This document outlines the procurement policy for supply chain operations, including vendor selection, purchase order management, and emergency procurement procedures.

## Vendor Selection Criteria
- Vendors must be pre-approved and registered in the Approved Vendor List (AVL)
- Minimum reliability score of 75% required for primary vendor status
- Vendors with reliability below 60% are placed on probation and require dual approval
- All new vendors require a 90-day trial period with enhanced monitoring

## Lead Time Requirements
- Standard procurement lead time: 3-5 business days
- Expedited procurement: 1-2 business days (20% cost premium applies)
- Emergency procurement: Same-day or next-day (40-60% cost premium applies)
- Air freight escalation: Authorized when sea/ground ETA exceeds SLA by >48 hours

## Emergency Procurement Authorization
When stockout risk is detected within 72 hours:
1. Procurement Agent initiates emergency vendor contact
2. Manager approval required for orders >$50,000
3. Alternative supplier activation requires Procurement Director approval for >$200,000
4. Split shipment authorized when single supplier cannot fulfill full quantity

## Supplier Diversification Rule
- No single supplier should represent >60% of critical component supply
- At least 2 pre-qualified backup suppliers must be maintained per category
- Quarterly supplier performance reviews are mandatory

## Cost Escalation Thresholds
- Up to 15% cost increase: Procurement Agent authorized
- 15-30% cost increase: Manager approval required
- >30% cost increase: Director approval required

## Documentation Requirements
- All purchase orders must include: item code, quantity, unit price, delivery date, SLA reference
- Emergency POs require written justification and manager co-sign
- Vendor communications must be logged in the procurement system within 24 hours
