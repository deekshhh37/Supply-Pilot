# Warehouse Operations Guidelines

## Inventory Management

### Safety Stock Policy
- Safety stock = (Max daily demand × Max lead time) - (Avg daily demand × Avg lead time)
- Minimum safety stock maintained: 7 days of average demand for critical items
- Reorder point triggered when: stock ≤ safety stock + demand during lead time

### Stock Transfer Procedures
- Inter-warehouse transfers authorized when: source has >30 days supply AND destination has <7 days supply
- Transfer lead time: 1-2 business days for regional, 3-5 days for cross-regional
- Emergency transfers: Same-day dispatch authorized for critical SLA items
- Transfer cost: $0.50/unit for regional, $1.20/unit for cross-regional

### Inventory Health Categories
- **Healthy**: ≥14 days of supply, above safety stock
- **Watch**: 7-14 days of supply, approaching safety stock
- **At Risk**: 3-7 days of supply, below safety stock threshold
- **Critical**: <3 days of supply, immediate action required
- **Overstock**: >60 days of supply, review for redistribution or clearance

### Overstock Management
- Items with >90 days supply flagged for promotion or transfer
- Transfer to high-demand warehouses before external clearance
- Promotional discount authorized up to 25% for items >120 days supply

## Warehouse Locations and Capacity
- **WH-001 (Main Distribution Center - Chicago)**: 500,000 sq ft, 48-hour processing
- **WH-002 (East Coast Hub - Newark)**: 200,000 sq ft, 24-hour processing
- **WH-003 (West Coast Hub - Los Angeles)**: 180,000 sq ft, 24-hour processing
- **WH-004 (Southeast Depot - Atlanta)**: 120,000 sq ft, 36-hour processing
- **WH-005 (Central Depot - Dallas)**: 150,000 sq ft, 36-hour processing

## Receiving and Putaway
- Inbound shipments must be counted and quality-checked within 4 hours of arrival
- Discrepancies >2% require vendor notification and hold for investigation
- Fast-moving items stored in pick zones; slow-moving in bulk storage
- Temperature-sensitive items require cold chain documentation
