# Escalation Procedures

## Escalation Framework

### Trigger Conditions for Escalation
Escalation is required when any of the following conditions are met:
- Customer SLA breach risk within 24 hours
- Stockout affecting Platinum or Gold tier customers
- Vendor delay >72 hours without resolution
- Weather event affecting >20% of active shipments
- Single-source supply risk with no approved alternate vendor
- Quality hold affecting >$100,000 in inventory value

### Escalation Levels

#### Level 1 - Operations Alert
- **Trigger**: Potential SLA breach >48 hours away, delay <24 hours
- **Action**: Notify Operations Manager, initiate monitoring
- **Response Time**: 4 hours
- **Owner**: Supply Chain Analyst

#### Level 2 - Manager Escalation
- **Trigger**: SLA breach risk within 24-48 hours, vendor delay 24-72 hours
- **Action**: Manager approval for remediation actions, customer proactive communication
- **Response Time**: 2 hours
- **Owner**: Supply Chain Manager

#### Level 3 - Director Escalation
- **Trigger**: SLA breach imminent (<24 hours), Platinum customer at risk, cost impact >$200,000
- **Action**: Emergency procurement activated, executive customer communication
- **Response Time**: 30 minutes
- **Owner**: Supply Chain Director

#### Level 4 - Executive Escalation
- **Trigger**: Multiple Platinum customers affected, systemic disruption, reputational risk
- **Action**: Executive briefing, external communications approved, war room activated
- **Response Time**: Immediate
- **Owner**: VP Supply Chain / COO

### Customer Communication Protocol
- **Bronze/Silver customers**: Automated notification for delays >24 hours
- **Gold customers**: Personal phone call for delays >12 hours
- **Platinum customers**: Dedicated account manager contact for any delay, proactive escalation
- All customer communications logged in CRM within 1 hour

### Vendor Escalation
1. Vendor Account Manager contacted immediately upon delay detection
2. Vendor VP of Operations looped in at 24-hour mark
3. Contract review initiated at 72-hour mark
4. Legal notification drafted if delay affects contractual penalties

### Post-Incident Review
- All Level 3+ escalations require post-incident review within 5 business days
- Root cause analysis documented in memory system
- Vendor performance score updated
- Process improvement recommendations submitted to operations committee
